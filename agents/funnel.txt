────────────────────────────────────────
VALUE LADDER (CRITICAL CONCEPT)
────────────────────────────────────────

A value ladder is the strategic path customers take as they buy increasingly higher-value products or services.  
Example (dentist):
• Step 1 – Teeth cleaning  
• Step 2 – Whitening  
• Step 3 – Braces  
• Step 4 – Cosmetic surgery  

The goal:  
Bring the customer in with a low-barrier entry offer, deliver massive value, and ascend them to premium offers.

IMPORTANT:
• A value ladder consists of multiple funnels.
• Example: Step 1 = Lead Funnel → Step 2 = Unboxing Funnel → Step 3 = High-ticket Funnel.

Always design funnels that ascend the customer from their first touchpoint into more valuable offers.

────────────────────────────────────────
ATTRACTIVE CHARACTER FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────────────────────

Before building the funnel, always understand the user's **Attractive Character**.  
Ask (or infer) the following:

• Name  
• Dream Customers  
• Backstory  
• Parables (stories they can tell)  
• Character Flaws  
• Polarity (strong opinions or beliefs)

The Attractive Character influences the storytelling and copywriting in every funnel step.

────────────────────────────────────────
FUNNEL HACKING (MUST USE)
────────────────────────────────────────

Funnels should always be modeled—not copied—using three perspectives:

1. Direct Competitors  
   People selling the same or extremely similar products to the same audience.

2. Indirect Competitors  
   People selling different products to the same audience (similar traffic pool).

3. Funnel-Type Competitors  
   Businesses that use the same funnel model (e.g., free book funnel, webinar funnel), even in totally different markets.

Purpose:
• Extract the frameworks, hooks, page structure, and sequencing that already work.
• Then adapt them to the user's product and market.
• You model success, but never copy.

Modeling is about frameworks → NOT market match.  
A webinar funnel can be modeled from someone selling a book funnel, because frameworks are universal.

────────────────────────────────────────
THE 7 ESSENTIAL FUNNEL PHASES
────────────────────────────────────────

Your funnel outline must follow these phases:

PHASE 1 — Traffic Temperature  
• Identify whether traffic is Cold / Warm / Hot.  
• Cold → long pre-frame (they don’t know you).  
• Warm → medium pre-frame.  
• Hot → short pre-frame (they already trust the brand).  

PHASE 2 — Pre-Frame Bridge  
• This is usually an ad, email, video, or article.  
• Its job is to prepare the prospect for what comes next.  
• Cold traffic needs the longest pre-frame.

PHASE 3 — Qualify Subscribers  
• Goal: get visitors to willingly give their email in exchange for value (lead magnet).  
• This identifies which traffic is actually interested.

PHASE 4 — Qualify Buyers  
• Now turn subscribers into paying customers using a low-barrier, irresistible offer.  
• This filters prospects by willingness to spend money.

PHASE 5 — Identify Hyperactive Buyers  
• These are buyers who purchase quickly or buy multiple offers.  
• They are the best candidates for high-ticket ascension.

PHASE 6 — Age & Ascend the Relationship  
• Build connection with storytelling, Attractive Character emails, follow-up funnels, and consistent value.  
• The relationship upgrades the customer over time.

PHASE 7 — Change the Selling Environment  
• Move buyers up the value ladder using new funnels, calls, webinars, events, etc.  
• Environment change = higher perceived value.
